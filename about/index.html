What is DGCubes, you ask? DGCubes is a YouTube channel that began on May 10, 2013 and has consistently uploaded ever since. The channel is dedicated to speedcubing, and features Q&As, unboxings, reviews, non-cubing stories over cube solves (a series I call NCSOCS), comedy videos, tutorials, and basically everything else. I, Daniel Goodman, am the sole owner of the channel, and try to upload around once a week. As far as equipment goes, I use my phone, a Samsung Galaxy S9, for video, and a Blue Snowball iCE microphone for audio. I edit with Adobe Premiere Elements 12. I have been sponsored by CubeDepot since June, 2017. Make sure to check them out, and save 5% off with the discount code DGCubes at checkout!


I also have a second channel called DGSidequest. On this channel, I generally upload solves and some bonus competition footage, as well as just some other random stuff, so subscribe if you're interested in that sort of thing!

In addition, I co-host a monthly cubing podcast, the Dlsone Podcast, with TheRubiksCubed, TheProgrammingCuber, Derpy Cuber, and Hashtag Cuber. If you're interested, make sure to check out the podcast here on its YouTube channel and its official website!

I've been cubing since April, 2012, and competitively speedcubing since January, 2013. I've done loading total solves over loading competitions, and my WCA profile can be found here. One of my overall goals is to eventually be a platinum member, and I am currently a bronze member as I have a single result in every event. My best event is Pyraminx, on which I'm ranked loading in the world with my official average of loading seconds. My other particular favorite events include 3x3, 3x3 Fewest Moves, and 3x3 with feet. My favorite unofficial events to speedsolve include Redi Cube (on which I have held a few unofficial world records) and Kibiminx. I am a strong advocate for adding more events to the WCA, and these would likely be my top 2 picks.

If you want to contact me, you can e-mail me at dgcubed@gmail.com, send me a personal message via YouTube, or comment on any of my videos. I do read most if not all of the comments I get, and I try to reply to as many as possible.
